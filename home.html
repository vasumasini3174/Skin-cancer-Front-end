About Skin Cancer

Skin cancer is the abnormal growth of skin cells, most often developing on skin exposed to the sun. Early detection and diagnosis are crucial for effective treatment and better outcomes.

  • Basal Cell Carcinoma (BCC): The most common type, usually appears as a slightly transparent bump on the skin. It rarely spreads but can cause local damage if untreated.
  • Squamous Cell Carcinoma (SCC): Often appears as a firm, red nodule or a flat lesion with a scaly, crusted surface. It can spread to other parts of the body if not treated early.
  • Melanoma: The most serious type, can develop anywhere on the body. It often appears as a new, unusual growth or a change in an existing mole. Early detection is critical as melanoma can spread rapidly.

How Our Prediction System Helps

Our Skin Cancer Prediction website uses advanced machine learning algorithms to analyze skin lesion images and provide an early indication of potential skin cancer. By uploading an image of a skin lesion, users can receive a preliminary assessment, which can help in:

  • Detecting skin cancer at an early stage, when treatment is most effective.
  • Raising awareness about suspicious skin changes that may require medical attention.
  • Encouraging timely consultation with healthcare professionals for further diagnosis and treatment.
